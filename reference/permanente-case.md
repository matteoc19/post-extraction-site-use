# Worked Case: Permanente Quarry, Santa Clara County, CA

A trace of the three passes applied to a real site — the candidates that lost, the scenario that cohered, and the governing-language research that dissolved it into something sharper and more useful.

**Note on how this case was actually run.** The original research did *not* front-load the gates. It ran the passes but discovered the Gate 3 / Gate 4 / Step 5 findings only at Pass 03, as a surprise. That is precisely the failure this skill now exists to prevent. Where a gate would have caught something earlier, it is flagged below.

---

## PASS 01 — Solution Space

### Gate 1 — Source Hierarchy

| Source class | Found? | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Operator's own project site | **Late** | `permanentequarry.com` — full public reclamation documentation. Found only after weeks of analysis built on news coverage. |
| Filed regulatory document | **Late** | The 1,869-page June 2023 Reclamation Plan Amendment. Everything load-bearing was in it the entire time: the 42/31.2 mcy fill split (Table 5), the 990 ft design-surface rationale, a commissioned regional soil-supply study, and a committed Imported Soils Management Plan. |
| Governing agency file | Yes | Santa Clara County Planning |
| Independent measurement | Yes | Google Earth polygon + elevation reads |
| **Existing statutory framework** | **Never searched** | California's Inert Debris Engineered Fill Operation (14 CCR 17388.3) — a permit class letting quarries accept fill *and charge tipping fees*. This is the exact mechanism Pass 02 would later "propose." |

**Lesson:** the two richest sources — the operator's own website and its own filed plan — were the last two found. The party with the strongest incentive to publish was the last one searched.

### Gate 3 — Negative Claim Protocol

Claim made: *"California lacks a CCDD-style fill regime."*
Search run to establish it: **none.**
Reality: IDEFO is the direct equivalent, including tipping fees.

One search would have caught this. It was never run because the claim felt obviously true. **This is exactly the failure the gate exists to force.**

### Gate 4 — Red Team

Question never asked: *"What would make this entire analysis worthless?"*

Answer, had it been asked: *"A statutory pathway and functioning market for quarry fill already exists — and the operator is a sophisticated materials company that sells to the very projects generating the spoil."*

Both were true. The gate was not asked precisely *because the analysis was going well.*

### Steps 1–5

**Step 1 — Physical.** 198-acre pit; 550–1,750 ft MSL; ~1,200 ft depth. Independent Google Earth measurement returned 225 ac — a ~12% variance from the RPA's 198, reconciled as the difference between full excavation disturbance and the defined reclamation area. Discrepancy stated, not silently resolved.

**Step 2 — Mandated or foreclosed.** **Fill is mandated.** The SMARA reclamation plan requires backfilling to a 990 ft design surface. Selenium and nickel contamination is *why* the void cannot be left open. Decisive, and it immediately narrows the candidate set.

**Step 3 — What is the void currently *for*?** Nothing that depends on it remaining a void.

> **Contrast — Thornton Quarry, Illinois.** Physically similar; opposite answer. Thornton is a Deep Tunnel/TARP stormwater reservoir (7.9 billion gallons, protecting ~500,000 people, ~$400M spent grouting it to hold water). **Filling that void would destroy several hundred million dollars of flood infrastructure.** Same typology, opposite correct answer. This step is cheap to ask and catastrophic to skip.

**Step 4 — Candidates generated.** Open space/trail · Groundwater recharge · Overburden solar · Ecological research · Fill/reclamation · Pumped hydro.

**Step 5 — Existing pathway check.** **Not performed at the time. This is the skill's most important addition.**

Performed retroactively: IDEFO already permits quarries to accept inert debris and soil as engineered fill and charge tipping fees, and can continue after a SMARA plan closes. One consulting firm alone reports engineering 11M+ yd³ of such fill. An active IDEFO CEQA process exists in Orange County.

**Effect:** does not kill the analysis, but reframes it. The mechanism is not novel. What remains is a question about *routing*, not invention.

### Pass 01 Output — ranked candidates

| Candidate | Champion | Blocker | Earliest start |
|---|---|---|---|
| Open space / trail | Midpen, Sierra Club | No acquisition funding; private ownership to 2065+ | ~2065 |
| Groundwater recharge | SCVWD (potential) | Contamination must reach non-detect | post-2040 |
| Overburden solar | Operator (revenue) | Community opposition; interconnection | 2030s |
| Ecological research | None identified | No proponent | post-2040 |
| Pumped hydro | None | Fill mandate forecloses it | never, as configured |
| **Fill / reclamation** | Operator (obligated) | Sourcing 31.2 mcy of import | **now** |

**Desirable vs. actionable.** Open space is the most desirable end state and the most likely eventual one — but it is a 40-year horizon with no committed funding. Fill is the only candidate actionable today, *because it is the one the site is already obligated to perform.* It does not foreclose open space; it shapes the landform open space would eventually occupy.

**Carried into Pass 02: Fill / reclamation.**

## PASS 02 — Scenario Definition

**Step 6 — Governing figures, from the RPA itself.**
- 42 mcy total pit backfill; **31.2 mcy required from off-site import** (Table 5, "balance needed from off-site import")
- Design surface: 990 ft amsl — *the lowest natural outlet in the surrounding topography, so the reclaimed surface cannot impound water.* A hydrological constraint, not an arbitrary target. Stating the reason materially strengthened the analysis.
- Early phases (heaviest fill) complete within five years of county approval

**Step 7 — Derivation, arithmetic shown.**

BART Silicon Valley Phase II, a 5-mile single-bore transit tunnel ~18 miles from the site:

```
Cross-section:        π × 24² ft            = 1,810 ft²   (48 ft excavated dia.)
Tunnel (bank):        1,810 × 26,400 ÷ 27   = 1,770,000 yd³
3 station boxes:      3 × (800×80×50) ÷ 27  =   355,000 yd³
Shafts / launch:                            =   100,000 yd³
Subtotal (bank)                             = 2,225,000 yd³
× 1.25 swell (Santa Clara alluvial clay)    = 2,781,000 yd³
Reported conservatively as                  ≈ 2.5M yd³      [DERIVED]
```

Caltrain grade separations (7 crossings, three cities): ~2.2M yd³ — **[ASSUMED]**, typological, no published cut volumes. Tagged and displayed distinctly from the derived figure.

**Step 8 — Parties and timeline.** BART tunnel boring: 2027–2033 (*not* underway, as first assumed). Caltrain crossings: staggered, one nearly complete, several unfunded, one undated. Total identified regional supply: ~8–10M yd³ against a 31.2M need. **Gap stated honestly** — identified supply is real but insufficient, which is itself an argument for deliberate routing rather than opportunistic capture.

**Pass 02 Output:** a coherent scenario — route regional capital-project spoil into the site's imported-fill stream before excavation contracts commit it elsewhere. Volumes match, geography works, windows overlap.

**Coherent is not the same as necessary.**

## PASS 03 — Governing Language & Feasibility

**Step 9 — The mechanism against governing language.**
California's IDEFO (14 CCR 17388.3) already codifies exactly what Pass 02 proposed: a quarry accepting imported fill and charging tipping fees, continuable after the SMARA plan closes. **The financial mechanism modeled from first principles is existing statute.**

**Step 10 — The counterparty's actual position.**
Heidelberg Materials sells aggregate, cement, and ready-mix *to the contractors building these projects*; aggregate operations at Permanente continue today. Their own RPA contains a commissioned four-county regional soil-supply study estimating 500k–1M yd³/yr receivable.

**This is not a party lacking visibility into regional material flows.** A two-year architect did not notice a supply–demand match that a multinational materials company's reclamation team missed. That was always the least likely explanation — and it went untested because the analysis was going well.

**Step 11 — What actually survives.**

The coordination premise dissolves. Something narrower and sharper remains:

> **A permitting-timeline risk.** The RPA is *not yet approved.* Until Santa Clara County approves it, Permanente cannot legally receive imported fill at all — no soils management plan, no IDEFO, no receiving operation. Meanwhile VTA's tunneling contract is let *before* boring begins in 2027, and contractors commit spoil destinations at bid.
>
> If the RPA is not approved before the region's largest excavation contracts are let, the largest single fill source of the decade is committed elsewhere by default — regardless of who wants it or what it would cost.

**This also relocates the lever.** The binding decision belongs to the **Santa Clara County Board of Supervisors** (RPA approval), not to VTA or Caltrain — agencies with no vote on a county reclamation permit. The original analysis had identified the wrong institution.

**Pass 03 Output: B — Narrowed finding.**

## What the analysis added vs. what was already there

**Already in the source material** (and initially mistaken for novel findings):
- The 31.2 mcy import need — RPA Table 5
- The argument that imported fill is *preferable* to on-site-only backfill — RPA §4.2
- A regional surplus-soil study estimating 500k–1M yd³/yr — the operator's own consultants
- A committed Imported Soils Management Plan, RWQCB-reviewed
- The VMT-reduction rationale — the operator's own words
- **And, unfound at the time: the IDEFO statutory pathway itself**

**What the analysis actually added:** the timing observation. A specific, checkable claim about a live county permitting decision with a hard external deadline. Not a fill mechanism (codified), not the fill volumes (published), not the regional supply (already studied by the operator).

## The transferable lesson

Every failure in this case was a failure of **not looking**, not a failure of reasoning. The reasoning on the material in hand was sound throughout. The gaps clustered with total predictability around three things:

1. **The primary actor's own publications** (operator website, filed plan)
2. **The existing legal and market landscape** (IDEFO)
3. **The analyst's own premise** (that a coordination gap existed at all)

A framework cannot make an analyst reason better. It can make the **unsearched territory visible** — which is what the four gates are for, and why they belong at the *start* of Pass 01 rather than arriving as a Pass 03 surprise.
